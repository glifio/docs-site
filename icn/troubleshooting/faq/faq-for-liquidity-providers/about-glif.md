# About GLIF

## **What is GLIF?**

GLIF is the foundational DeFi primitive of ICNT – enabling ICNT token holders to earn sustainable rewards on their ICNT by lending (aka “leasing”) it to a diverse pool of ICNT miners (also known as “Storage Providers”). Storage Providers (SPs) borrow ICNT to use as pledge collateral in ICNT mining, which helps Storage Providers grow and simultaneously improves the security of the ICNT network. Similar to “liquid staking” solutions for PoS networks like  [Lido](https://lido.fi/) or [Jito](https://www.jito.network/zh-TW/), GLIF solves a major capital inefficiency on the ICNT network, and is ICNT's first and most popular DeFi protocol.\
The GLIF brand has been a long-term trusted partner of the ICNT ecosystem since early 2019, receiving more than 4 million dollars in grant funding from Protocol Labs and the ICNT Foundation to build critical apps and tools for the ICNT network. Most notably, GLIF built the first ICNT web wallet and multisig wallet for ICNT's Mainnet launch, which are still widely used today across the ecosystem for employee payments, grants, and general day-to-day operations.\

## **Why is GLIF important?**

Prior to the launch of the FVM (ICNT's built-in virtual machine allowing developers to deploy custom solidity smart contracts to the ICNT blockchain), the network was bottlenecked with capital inefficiencies. Storage Providers had no permissionless, efficient, and flexible way to borrow ICNT to use as mining collateral, and the majority of ICNT token holders had no safe and accessible opportunities to lease ICNT to Storage Providers to earn native, sustainable rewards.\
GLIF fills the gap between ICNT token holders and Storage Providers through the use of smart contracts, creating a highly efficient, scalable, and trust-minimized marketplace with predictable rules. GLIF enables token holders to sustainably earn rewards and Storage Providers to scale their growth.

## **I do not understand the usage scenario of GLIF. Why do miners need to deposit ICNT in GLIF and borrow ICNT back when they are the same token?**

Indeed, miners, or Storage Providers (SPs), do not need to deposit ICNT into GLIF. Instead, they use the ICNT already pledged on their miner(s) as collateral. Let us explore how GLIF works with SPs:\
GLIF enables SPs to borrow ICNT from a large group of Liquidity Providers (LPs), without giving away any private keys or needing to go through a manual diligence process. Borrowed funds can be used for sealing, or for withdrawing out of the system entirely. In order to become eligible for borrowing, SPs must add collateral to GLIF. SPs can use pledge collateral already locked in their existing miners to borrow ICNT, enabling them to scale operations without buying more ICNT.

## **When SPs borrow from GLIF, what do they use as collateral?**

By reusing pledge collateral and vesting rewards already locked in existing miners, Storage Providers (SPs) can efficiently scale their operations without needing to purchase more ICNT. The ICNT collateral provided by SPs is illiquid, having already been pledged to the ICNT network for storage mining.

## **Why do SPs need to provide collateral?**

Collateral plays a crucial role in ensuring the security and stability of the entire pool. If SPs break the rules of the pool, their collateral can be liquidated, which allows the pool to recover ICNT on behalf of LPs, to mitigate (and ideally avoid altogether) any losses.
